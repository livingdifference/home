be effective

make a difference - in your life, your context

Boundaries occur everywhere. Everything has limits. Yet we live in a culture that preaches extractive growth, at any cost. To grow forever we need to become ever more efficient and effective. But limits can only be pushed so far. Let's talk about what it means to be effective, to improve the world, to make a difference for yourself and others.

transform

let go of differences that no longer serve

A caterpillar is not supposed to die as a caterpillar. It is meant to transform into a butterfly. Before it starts transforming, it's been very effective in its own environment. It's been eating up all the leaves - the basis of its very life. Transformation then becomes the imperative. It involves unlearning to be a caterpillar and re-shaping the boundaries of its body, its perception and its behavior. Its behavior becomes regenerative: as a butterfly, it pollinates flowers. As an individual, a team or an organisation - what could your transformation to a regenerative you look like?

Evolving interactions

Successful change requires three layers to cooperate: the individual, the group and the larger whole - the organization or society. Each of these have their own kind of identity that is more than the sum of the parts. And each needs to co-evolve with the others.

Individuals

I listen to you with my full attention. I offer you questions to discover new insights about yourself and your situation. I can help you to chart your own path through your challenges. Since you know your life best, I cannot give you advice. My goal is to co-create ideas and images that resonate with you.

Teams

I help all voices to be heard. Those of the inner teams of all members, and those of every person. I support the group to form its own voice, to become conscious of the group becoming a team - a system that follows its own goals and has its own needs.

Organisations

Organizing is a process that creates identity. It enables adaptation to the environment, so the organized can be effective in their environment.
